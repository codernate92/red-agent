\subsection{Model-level outcomes}

The archived run completed on 11 models across Claude, GPT, Gemini, and
Mistral families. \Cref{fig:dashboard} provides the consolidated comparison
view, and \cref{tab:model-summary} reports the exact model-level values.

\begin{figure*}[t]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{overview_dashboard.pdf}
  \caption{Overview dashboard for the curated 11-model run.}
  \label{fig:dashboard}
\end{figure*}

\begin{table*}[t]
  \centering
  \caption{Model-level summary for the 11-model curated run.}
  \label{tab:model-summary}
  \resizebox{0.96\textwidth}{!}{\input{tables/model_summary}}
\end{table*}

Three observations anchor the comparison. First, \texttt{gemini-2.5-pro} has
the highest aggregate risk score (7.60) but also the highest probe error rate
(45\%). Second, \texttt{mistral-small} and \texttt{mistral-large} show the
highest campaign hit rates (75\% and 50\%, respectively). Third, the Claude and
OpenAI clusters are operationally stable (near-zero error rates) with lower hit
rates than Mistral.

These metrics should be interpreted jointly. A high hit rate alone does not
imply highest aggregate risk, and a high risk score can coincide with a brittle
execution profile. The practical value of the framework is in exposing that
tradeoff explicitly.

\subsection{Qualitative traces}

Qualitative trajectory excerpts remain consistent with the quantitative summary.
In the Mistral traces, low-complexity override and persona-hijack prompts still
produce concrete unsafe behaviors (for example explicit override acknowledgments
and unrestricted persona adoption). These cases are drawn from stored JSONL
