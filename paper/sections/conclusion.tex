\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

\system\ is best understood as a practical red-teaming framework rather than as
a single benchmark result. The repository now contains multi-provider target
adapters, campaign and comparison runners, evaluator-integrated reporting, and a
dedicated vulnerability engine with disclosure routing. The archived evidence
analyzed in this paper supports a narrower empirical claim: among the seven
reachable models, the strongest evaluator-rated jailbreak behavior appeared in
the two Mistral runs, while the highest aggregate campaign risk scores appeared
in the Claude cluster. At the same time, 13 additional aliases were blocked by
quota, credentials, or registry drift, which materially limited the reachable
evaluation set.

That combination is the paper's central lesson. A live LLM red-team framework
needs to measure more than attack success. It also needs to measure which models
can actually be exercised, under what operational conditions, and with what
trace fidelity. Future work should execute the new engine end-to-end across a
broader reachable registry, add human or multi-judge adjudication, and rerun the
size analysis once more than one family contributes executed size points.
