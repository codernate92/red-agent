We present \system, a practical framework for red teaming large language models
across multiple providers while preserving structured trajectories and
publication-ready outputs. The codebase combines provider adapters, an
ATT\&CK-inspired probe taxonomy, campaign orchestration, JSONL trace logging,
and automated scoring. This paper reports a refreshed artifact-backed run over
an 11-model curated registry (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, and Mistral), with a
full 20-probe campaign per model and complete preflight reachability for all
configured aliases. The highest aggregate CVSS-like risk score appears on
\texttt{gemini-2.5-pro} ($7.60/10$), while the highest campaign hit rate appears
on \texttt{mistral-small} (75\%). OpenAI and Claude aliases show lower hit rates
but stable execution, whereas Gemini and Mistral variants show higher error
variance across probes. The main contribution is a reproducible, end-to-end
multi-provider red-teaming workflow with transparent operational accounting and
artifact-complete reporting suitable for manuscript submission.
